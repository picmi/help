# Understanding streamlined engagement

PICMI supports streamlined engagement across employment and service ecosystems.

The same platform model can be used to:

- recruit and place workers
- intake and onboard service participants
- manage provider or contractor service engagements
- run programme participation workflows

## Core approach

PICMI helps organisations standardise engagement into a repeatable flow:

1. Define the opportunity and intake configuration
2. Invite people or open intake
3. Run workflow-driven information collection and declarations
4. Finalise agreement terms
5. Transition into active placement or participation

## Why this matters

A unified engagement model reduces operational friction:

- fewer handoff errors between teams
- more consistent compliance and documentation
- faster placement and activation decisions
- better visibility across opportunities

## Employment and services examples

### Employment scenario

An organisation publishes a seasonal workforce opportunity, invites returning workers, and uses intake rules to manage capacity and offer flow.

### Services scenario

A provider opens intake for support service participation, collects required information through workflows, and allocates participants into active service engagements.
